Maybe you've seen the xkcd comic? For those who haven't:
Did you know that Python 3.0 and later have such a module? Open up your favorite Python terminal, and try this:
import antigravity
Python has, from day one, been a light-hearted language. Read the online Python documentation long enough, and you'll notice a number of Monty Python references, in honor of Python's namesake. The people behind Python (including the Benevolent Dictator for Life) have designed it to be a simple and powerful language
Where am I going with this? I am one of the many people who think Python is an awesome language, with an entertaining history. The purpose of the CSSE120 Tangents series is to give me a place to preach about some of the inner workings of Python that the Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology Computer Science curriculum won't address directly for a while. There are some topics that I will try to avoid talking about, because you will find out about very closely related material soon enough.

I hope that some Computer Science majors enjoy reading about Python.
